From Passion to Portfolio: My Journey into Full-Stack Development

Published: 9th December 2024, 14:30

I recently made the decision to try my hand at full-stack development—a journey fuelled by my love for problem-solving, technology and creating meaningful projects. With a background in transformation programme management, I’ve always enjoyed collaborating with others to deliver seemingly magical solutions, streamlining processes and delivering innovative, but I wanted to take this a step further by building the solutions myself.

Alongside this transition, I’ve embraced my passion for self-hosting, maintaining a Proxmox server that powers a range of services—from media streaming to file storage. This hands-on experience has given me a deep appreciation for managing systems and making the most of the tools available to developers today.

My journey has also involved merging my hobbies with my development work. Take RideFix, for example—a cycling maintenance app designed to gamify bike care while providing users with practical tools to track their bike’s condition. The app is a reflection of my interests in cycling, gamification, and creating software that solves real problems.

These experiences form the backbone of my portfolio and represent what I bring to full-stack development: an eagerness to learn, a strong technical foundation, and a passion for building tools that make life easier and more enjoyable. If you’d like to learn more about my projects, check out my posts on self-hosting, RideFix, and how I got started in full-stack development.
