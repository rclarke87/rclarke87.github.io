Why do I need RideFixr?

Bike shop labour can cost anywhere from £60 to £100 per hour. Simple fixes like changing a flat, adjusting brakes, or cleaning your chain can save you hundreds each year—and they don’t require expensive tools.

Working on your own bike means no hassle of getting to the mechanic. Well-timed maintenance prevents minor issues from becoming costly overhauls, keeping your bike in good shape and your bills low. Never forget a service again with our tailored schedules.

Bikes are an energy-efficient and eco-friendly way to get around, but even they have an environmental impact during manufacturing and distribution. The best way to protect the environment is by making your bike last longer and reducing the need for replacements and parts.

A well-maintained bike not only rides smoother but can also fetch a better price if you ever decide to upgrade or sell—up, though let's be honest, no one ever really gives up on cycling, right?

Knowing how your bike works makes you a better rider, keeps you safe, and gives you peace of mind on the open road. With the right knowledge, you’ll never have to worry about getting stranded again.

Every DIY repair adds up. Here’s what bike shops charge for common fixes:
  • Flat tyre: £10-£20 per tyre
  • Brake adjustment: £15-£30
  • Chain clean/lube: £10-£20
  • Gear tuning: £20-£40
  • Brake pad replacement: £20-£40
By learning these basics, you’ll keep more money in your pocket—and more miles on your bike.
